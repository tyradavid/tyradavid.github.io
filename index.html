About Me

I am a third-year student completing my Bachelor of Commerce at McGill University’s Desautels Faculty of Management. With specializations in Business Analytics and Information Systems, my skillset includes analytical tools, statistical analysis, data visualization, database management, and more recently, machine learning (R, Python, SQL, Tableau, Power BI, Excel).

I believe in an interdisciplinary approach of combining technical skills and my love for technology to solve problems and generate recommendations. The future involves no longer relying on intuition, but instead, using big data and machine learning for predictive purposes. Analytics gives us the leverage to understand the world, solve pressing problems, and ultimately improve the world, through data!

Five years ago, I began working as a lifeguard, after having a passion for swimming since I was a child. I was driven to go further and challenge myself, and developed a desire to share my knowledge with others while perfecting my skills. I am now implicated with the Lifesaving and teach and certify candidates as a National Lifeguard, in CPR and first aid.

I am also passionate about music and have been playing the clarinet for nine years now. I started off in my high school Honour Band and Jazz Band, and then started playing for McGill’s Symphonic Band Club. Recently, due to the global pandemic, I got involved with the Virtual Concert Band, a virtual band that unites musicians of all skill-levels, ages, and from all countries to share their passion for music.

Projects

Urban Street Scenes Object Detection

This project used Single Shot Detector CNN model to create an object detection web application that takes as input an image of an outdoor urban street scene and outputs class names and bounded boxes around vehicles, roads, buildings and people.

Impact of Potential New Legislation on Airbnb Availability in Montreal

This project examines and graphs Airbnb data from large North American cities to predict how Airbnb activity in Montreal can be impacted by potential new gentrification legislation.

Predicting the Success of Kickstarter Projects

This project creates a prediction model that evaluates which factors of a Kickstarter campaign influence their success rate the most to predict if a current Kickstarter project succeeds to be funded.

Microsoft’s Discover AI Challenge: Smarter and Sustainable Economies

This design project explores how retail can use AI and analytics to provide efficient, personalized retail experience, while optimizing and improving sustainability in the supply chain.
